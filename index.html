First Nations Australia - A Historical Overview

65,000+ Years Ago

The First Peoples of Australia arrive, developing diverse cultures and languages across the continent. They establish complex societies, sustainable land management practices, and rich spiritual traditions.

1788

British colonization begins with the arrival of the First Fleet in Sydney. This marks the start of a long period of dispossession, conflict, and the introduction of diseases that devastate First Nations communities.

1901

The Commonwealth of Australia is established. The Australian Constitution excludes Aboriginal people from being counted in the census and allows laws to be made that discriminate against them.

1930s - 1970s

The Stolen Generations. Government policies lead to the forced removal of many Aboriginal children from their families, causing lasting trauma and cultural disruption.

1967

A landmark referendum sees over 90% of Australians vote to amend the Constitution to include Aboriginal people in the census and give the Commonwealth Parliament the power to legislate for Aboriginal people.

1970s - Present

Land rights movements gain momentum, leading to the recognition of Aboriginal land ownership in some areas. Continued efforts towards reconciliation, self-determination, and addressing ongoing social and economic inequalities.

2008

Prime Minister Kevin Rudd delivers a formal Apology to the Stolen Generations on behalf of the Australian Government.

2023

A referendum on a proposed Indigenous Voice to Parliament is held. The proposal is not accepted.
